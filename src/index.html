Critical Thinking and Media Literacy

In today’s world, we receive a lot of information every day — on TV, social media, and the internet. That’s why I believe critical thinking and media literacy are very important skills.For me, critical thinking means not accepting everything as true. I try to ask questions like: Who is saying this? Why are they saying it? Is there evidence? This helps me to avoid fake news and make better decisions. Media literacy is also important because it helps me understand how media works. Sometimes, media shows only one side of the story or tries to make us feel emotions to sell something or change our opinion. With media literacy, I can see these tricks and think for myself. In conclusion, these skills help me to be more careful, more curious, and more independent when I read or watch something. I don’t believe everything. I think first.

Crime and Punishment

I believe that crime and punishment are not just about laws. They are also about people, choices, and consequences.Sometimes, people make bad decisions because they are in a difficult situation. That doesn’t make the crime right, but it makes me think more deeply.Should we punish or help them Punishment is important to protect society, but I also think people can learn and change. For example, education in prison can help someone become a better person after making a mistake. In my opinion, the best justice is not only punishment — it is also giving a chance to start again.
